When and how did you become a Christian?

Summer of 2007 during a youth group camp to the Lake District. Having started going to youth group in order to keep up with friends from school, I went to the camp a few times to enjoy the activities; walking, sailing etc; but never applied the talks to myself. One evening, the third year I was there, I was particularly struck be the lyrics of the songs we had sung so talked about them with a friend.

A couple of evenings later there was a time of reflection after one of the talks and God softened my heart to Him. I know that it was not anything I had achieved, or that the talk or the band had been particularly good that night, but that God had chosen that moment to reveal himself to me. I understood that I knew God existed, then just had to deal with what that meant for me, my life, my view of the world and my place in it.

I prayed about what I'd learnt with the leader of the week and the same friend I had talked to previously in the hope of getting some idea of where to go from here. Returning home that year felt much the same as any other. My family were not Christians so going back to no communal exposure to God felt normal. It took a long time to decide to start going to church, to CU and to actually listen to what I needed to learn.

My walk since has been very bumpy, I feel I have grown slowly and fallen often, but the opportunity to see how much God has changed me, to be able to look back at my life before I knew Christ and compare it with where He has brought be thus far feels like an enormous privilege.

How do you think that you have grown as a Christian since you came to university?

Having only been to church at home for a few years before coming to university, I think I have grown most in my appreciation for God's family in the Church. The opportunity to experience church life in somewhere completely new was a big learning challenge. I realised that there was vast areas of Godly living I hadn't realised I knew nothing about.

I was involved in a reasonably long term relationship and reluctantly learnt about prioritising God above everything. And have grown in my understanding of God, the gifts he has given me and the ways in which I can use them for Him.

What would you say are you main gifts and strengths?

The way in which I have felt most able to bless the church is through behind-the-scenes serving. I feel that I have been able to use my skills in things like computing to serve during worship and in the day to day running of a church. I have had some opportunities to lead groups and be involved in youth ministry and I think that my science background and own walk has helped me witness to teenagers struggling with the questions and doubts that often arise at that age.

What opportunities have you had to make use of these gifts so far?

I've been lucky enough to serve as a leader for a few years on the camp where I became a Christian. Doing things like sound and lyrics for talks, multimedia like videos and the website and leading discussion groups and seminars there. I've served on the PA rota at City for a year or so and have led a small group for the CU. Many of these opportunities were difficult learning experiences but I feel that they have been invaluable in my walk with God, learning to trust Him in the gifts that He gives me and learning that they are from Him and for Him.

What would you say are your main weaknesses?

Since becoming a Christian, I have seen myself fall in many areas of life and give into temptation. I feel that I have struggled particularly in the past with motivation to trust God when I feel distant from Him. The self exaggerating spiral of feeling distant so not seeking Him, so feeling more distant etc.

In the more practical areas, I struggle considerably with sharing my walk with others. I have a great tendency to not seek help until just after it gets to be a big problem in my life. I tend to justify this by convincing myself that since I never confided in anyone about Godly matters whilst at home that there is no need to do it in the rest of my life.

In certain areas of my life, I am also fantastically disorganised. Though this usually only goes so far as forgetting birthdays or exams, I have difficulty asking God for help since I find ways of justifying that too. "I'm able to be flexible with other people and fit into their schedule if I don't know what I'm supposed to be doing when myself".
